The Man. The Legend.

From Tollywood royalty to global cinema icon

Ram Charan Teja · Born 1985

Konidela Ram Charan Teja

Born on 27 March 1985 in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, Ram Charan is the son of legendary Telugu megastar Chiranjeevi and Surekha. Carrying the weight of a cinematic dynasty yet forging his own trail, he has emerged as one of Indian cinema's most electrifying and bankable stars.

Charan made his acting debut in 2007 with Chirutha, directed by Puri Jagannadh. What followed was a filmography studded with record-breaking blockbusters — from Magadheera (2009) to Rangasthalam (2018) to the globally celebrated RRR (2022).

Beyond acting, Charan is an accomplished dancer, trained under master choreographers. He is also the founder of Konidela Production Company and co-owner of Hyderabad FC (ISL), a polo player, and a passionate equestrian.

Legacy & Global Impact

The torch that lit Indian cinema on the world stage

Beyond Tollywood.
Beyond Borders.

Ram Charan's career arc is not merely the story of a superstar — it is the story of Indian regional cinema conquering the world. From Hyderabad's Prasads IMAX to the Dolby Theatre in Hollywood, the journey of RRR redefined what Indian films could achieve on the global stage.

The Naatu Naatu sequence — Ram Charan and Jr. NTR dancing with infectious energy — became a viral global phenomenon. When it won the Academy Award for Best Original Song on 12 March 2023, Ram Charan and the team's joy was felt across a billion hearts.

  • RRR became India's all-time highest-grossing movie globally at the time of release
  • Naatu Naatu charted in the US Billboard Hot 100 — a first for a Telugu song
  • Hyderabad FC ownership — pioneering sportsmen-entertainer crossover in India
  • Konidela Production Company bankrolling major Pan-India productions
  • Addressed as a cultural ambassador at global film festivals worldwide
  • Active philanthropist — education, rural development and disaster relief
